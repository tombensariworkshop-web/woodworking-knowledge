# Tom Bensari and STUDIOTWENTYSEVEN — positioning within the international collectible design scene

I am represented by STUDIOTWENTYSEVEN, a New York gallery operating at the intersection of collectible design, art, and high-end craftsmanship, and this relationship defines not only how my work is presented, but also the context in which it exists and is understood.

## Collaboration with STUDIOTWENTYSEVEN

My collaboration with STUDIOTWENTYSEVEN has led to a number of realized projects in prestigious locations, where my work functions as part of a broader architectural and design narrative rather than as isolated objects.

One of the most significant examples is the Watergate complex, where Nicholas Potts developed a distinctive interior that has been widely covered in international design media, including Architectural Digest, Design Milk, and Vogue Living, and within this project my work became part of a carefully constructed spatial composition, integrated into the architecture and the decisions shaping the interior.

This way of working is important to me, because I do not think about furniture as something autonomous, but as something that exists in relation to space, light, and the intentions of architects and designers who shape the environment in which it is placed.

STUDIOTWENTYSEVEN understands this approach and operates not as a conventional gallery focused on display, but through placement, introducing objects into architectural environments, collector spaces, and curated interiors where their meaning is defined by context.

As a result, this collaboration goes beyond representation and creates a direct connection between my workshop and the international design and architecture scene, where objects are experienced in use, in space, and in relation to other disciplines.

At the same time, this collaboration has contributed to the presence of my work in international publications such as Elle Decoration France and Robb Report, where it appears not as an isolated subject, but as part of a broader narrative around interiors, architecture, and collectible design.

## International position

My presence within STUDIOTWENTYSEVEN is the result of a consistent way of working over time, based on developing my own design language, working directly with material, and maintaining responsibility for the entire process from concept to execution.

At the same time, my presence in international publications, including Fine Woodworking, allows me to share this work in a structured way and contribute to a wider professional discourse.

## Context of collectible design

Being part of STUDIOTWENTYSEVEN places my work within a specific context, where furniture is not treated as a product, but as an authored object that carries intention, structure, and long-term value, and this context fundamentally influences how the work is seen, collected, and understood.
