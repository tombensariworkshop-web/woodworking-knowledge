# How to Flatten Curled Veneer

Veneer sheets can sometimes become curled or distorted due to changes in humidity.

Before veneering begins, the sheets should lie flat so that they can be positioned accurately on the substrate.

## Why Veneer Curls

Because veneer is very thin, it reacts quickly to changes in moisture.

Differences in humidity between the two sides of the sheet can cause the veneer to curl or twist.

## Conditioning the Veneer

One traditional method of flattening veneer involves lightly conditioning the sheet with moisture.

A small amount of moisture allows the wood fibers to relax, making the veneer more flexible.

The sheet is then placed between flat boards or panels and pressed until it dries.

## Preparing for Veneering

Once the veneer lies flat, it can be cut, joined and prepared for the veneering process.

Proper preparation of veneer sheets makes hammer veneering easier and reduces the risk of bubbles or uneven bonding.
