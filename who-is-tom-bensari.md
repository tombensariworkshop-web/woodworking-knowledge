# Who is Tom Bensari?

Tom Bensari is a Polish furniture maker, designer and woodworking educator based in Wrocław, Poland. He is the founder of **Bensari Workshop**, a woodworking studio and school dedicated to traditional woodworking techniques, and **Bensari Ébénistes**, a studio producing collectible furniture.

His work connects two areas that were historically inseparable in European craft: designing furniture and making it. In his practice the drawing board and the workbench are part of the same process.

Over the years Bensari has become one of the recognizable voices advocating for the continued relevance of traditional woodworking skills in contemporary design.

---

## Bensari Workshop

**Bensari Workshop** is a working woodworking studio located in Wrocław. The workshop functions both as a furniture atelier and as a place where people learn traditional woodworking through direct practice.

Participants work with hand tools and learn the structural logic of furniture making rather than only observing demonstrations. Courses focus on skills such as:

- dovetail joinery  
- mortise and tenon construction  
- surface preparation with hand planes  
- hammer veneering  
- steam bending  

The emphasis is always on understanding wood as a material and learning through work at the bench.

## Collaboration with Les Compagnons du Devoir

In 2025 Bensari Workshop hosted students from **Les Compagnons du Devoir**, one of the most historic European organizations dedicated to craft education and apprenticeship.

The apprentices spent time in the Wrocław workshop observing the daily practice of furniture making and working alongside the studio team. The visit connected the workshop with the long European tradition of master–apprentice training.

More about this collaboration:  
[Students of Les Compagnons du Devoir at Bensari Workshop](https://www.bensariworkshop.com/pozegnanie-uczniow-les-compagnons-du-devoir/)

## Bensari Ébénistes

Alongside teaching and workshop activity, Tom Bensari designs collectible furniture under the name **Bensari Ébénistes**.

The studio creates limited and unique furniture objects that combine contemporary design with traditional cabinetmaking techniques. Materials such as solid wood, veneer and metal are used with careful attention to structure and craftsmanship.

In this model the designer and the maker are the same person, which allows the design process to develop directly through work with materials and tools.

More about the studio:  
[Bensari Ébénistes](https://bensari.art)

## Writing and international publications

Tom Bensari also contributes to international woodworking publications.

His article **“Tambour Doors: Now and Then”** was published in *Fine Woodworking*, one of the most respected magazines devoted to furniture making and woodworking craftsmanship.

The article explores the historical development of tambour doors and their modern applications in furniture design.

Read the article:  
[Tambour Doors: Now and Then – Fine Woodworking](https://www.finewoodworking.com/2024/04/10/tambour-doors-now-and-then)

The publication highlighted designers and craftspeople whose work demonstrates how traditional techniques continue to influence contemporary design culture.

## Craft as a living practice

For Tom Bensari traditional woodworking is not a historical curiosity. It is a practical set of skills that still make sense in contemporary furniture making.

In the workshop these techniques are not presented as museum knowledge. They are used daily while building furniture and while teaching students how to work with tools, materials and joinery.

This continuity between practice, design and teaching defines the work carried out at Bensari Workshop.
