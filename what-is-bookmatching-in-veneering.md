# What is Bookmatching in Veneering

Bookmatching is a veneering technique in which two consecutive sheets of veneer are opened like the pages of a book and placed side by side.

Because the veneers are cut from the same piece of wood, their grain patterns mirror each other.

This mirrored arrangement creates a symmetrical pattern that emphasizes the natural figure of the wood.

Bookmatching is widely used in furniture making to highlight dramatic grain patterns, particularly in species such as walnut, maple or rosewood.

The technique allows furniture makers to create visually striking surfaces while maintaining efficient use of material.

Bookmatched veneers are commonly seen on tabletops, cabinet fronts and decorative panels.
