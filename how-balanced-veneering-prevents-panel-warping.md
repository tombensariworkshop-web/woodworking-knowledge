# How Balanced Veneering Prevents Panel Warping

One of the important principles in traditional veneered panel construction is balanced veneering.

This means that veneer is applied to both sides of a panel rather than only to the visible surface.

## Why Panels Can Warp

Wood reacts to changes in humidity by expanding and contracting across the grain.

If veneer is applied to only one side of a panel, the two faces of the panel may react differently to moisture changes. This imbalance can introduce internal stresses that cause the panel to warp or cup.

## Balanced Construction

Applying veneer to both sides of the panel helps equalize these stresses.

Because both surfaces respond similarly to environmental changes, the panel remains more stable over time.

In traditional cabinetmaking, even when one side of the panel is not visible, a backing veneer is often applied to maintain structural balance.

## Veneering as Structural Design

This principle shows that veneering is not only decorative but also structural.

Furniture makers use veneer not only to create beautiful surfaces but also to control how wooden panels behave as environmental conditions change.
