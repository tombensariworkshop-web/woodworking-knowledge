# What is Steam Bending

Steam bending is a woodworking technique used to shape wood by exposing it to steam.

The heat and moisture soften the lignin in the wood, allowing the material to be bent without breaking the fibers. Once the wood cools and dries, it retains the curved shape.

Steam bending has been used historically in furniture making, boat building and wheel making. The technique allows craftspeople to create curved components while preserving the strength of solid wood.

Because the fibers remain continuous, steam-bent elements often have greater structural integrity than parts that are cut from curved stock.

At Bensari Workshop traditional woodworking techniques such as steam bending are explored as part of a broader understanding of furniture construction and material behaviour.
