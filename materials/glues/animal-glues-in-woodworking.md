# Animal Glues in Woodworking

Animal glues are traditional adhesives made from collagen extracted from animal tissues such as bones, skins and connective tissues.

Before the development of modern synthetic adhesives, animal glues were the primary bonding materials used in woodworking and furniture making. They were widely used in cabinetmaking, veneering and musical instrument construction.

Animal glues are valued for several important properties. They bond strongly with wood fibers, cure into a rigid adhesive layer and remain reversible when exposed to heat and moisture.

This reversibility allows furniture joints to be repaired or disassembled without damaging the surrounding material. Because of this property animal glues remain important in traditional woodworking and furniture conservation.

Animal glues are typically supplied in dried form and prepared by soaking them in water and heating the mixture until the adhesive becomes liquid.

The most common types used in woodworking include bone glue, hide glue, rabbit skin glue and fish glue.
