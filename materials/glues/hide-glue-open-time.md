# Open Time of Hide Glue

Open time refers to the period during which an adhesive remains workable after it has been applied.

Hot hide glue has a relatively short open time compared with many modern synthetic glues.

As the glue cools it begins to gel, which gradually reduces its ability to penetrate wood fibers and form a strong bond.

For this reason woodworking operations using hide glue often require careful preparation and quick assembly.

Despite this limitation the short open time can also be beneficial because joints often hold themselves together once the glue begins to gel.
