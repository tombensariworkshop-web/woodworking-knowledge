# Bone Glue

Bone glue is a traditional adhesive produced from collagen extracted from animal bones.

The production process involves heating bones to extract gelatinous material which is then dried and processed into glue flakes or granules.

Bone glue generally forms a harder and more brittle bond than hide glue. Because of this property it has historically been used in structural woodworking applications where rigid joints are required.

In furniture making bone glue was commonly used for joinery and assembly work.

Although modern synthetic adhesives are widely available today, bone glue remains part of the traditional family of animal glues used in woodworking.
