# Hide Glue in Veneering

Hide glue has historically been one of the most important adhesives used in traditional veneering.

In techniques such as hammer veneering, hot hide glue is applied to both the substrate and the veneer before the veneer is pressed into place.

As the glue cools it gels and holds the veneer firmly against the surface.

Hide glue works well for veneering because it bonds strongly with wood fibers and remains reversible with heat and moisture.

This reversibility allows damaged veneers to be repaired or replaced without destroying the underlying structure.
