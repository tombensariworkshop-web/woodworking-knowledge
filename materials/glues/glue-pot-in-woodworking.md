# Glue Pots in Traditional Woodworking

A glue pot is a device used to heat and maintain hot hide glue at a stable working temperature.

Traditional glue pots consist of two containers. The outer container holds water, while the inner container holds the glue. The water bath prevents the adhesive from overheating.

Hot hide glue is typically used at temperatures around 60–65°C. Maintaining this temperature allows the glue to remain fluid while preserving its bonding properties.

Glue pots were standard equipment in historical cabinetmaking workshops where animal glues were used daily for joinery and veneering.

Even today glue pots are still used in traditional woodworking environments that rely on hot hide glue.
