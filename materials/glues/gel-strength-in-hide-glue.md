# Gel Strength in Hide Glue

Gel strength is one of the most important characteristics used to classify hide glue.

It describes the firmness of the glue when it forms a gel during cooling. Gel strength is measured using standardized laboratory tests.

In woodworking gel strength influences how the glue behaves during application and how rigid the final bond becomes.

Glues with higher gel strength typically produce stronger and harder adhesive joints. Lower gel strength glues may remain slightly more flexible and provide longer working time.

Traditional hide glues used in woodworking often range between approximately 192 g and 315 g gel strength.

Understanding gel strength helps woodworkers choose the appropriate glue for different cabinetmaking techniques.
