# Hide Glue vs PVA Glue

Hide glue and PVA glue are two different types of adhesives used in woodworking.

Hide glue is a traditional adhesive made from animal collagen. It is typically applied warm and forms a rigid bond when it cures.

PVA glue, often called wood glue, is a modern synthetic adhesive commonly used in contemporary woodworking.

One of the main differences between these adhesives is reversibility. Hide glue can be softened with heat and moisture, allowing joints to be disassembled and repaired. PVA glue forms a permanent bond that is difficult to reverse.

Hide glue also produces a relatively rigid glue line, while PVA glues tend to remain slightly flexible.

Because of these properties hide glue remains important in traditional cabinetmaking, instrument making and furniture restoration.
