# Preparing Hot Hide Glue

Hot hide glue is traditionally prepared from dried glue granules.

The preparation process begins by soaking the granules in water so that they absorb moisture and swell.

After soaking, the glue is gently heated in a glue pot until it becomes liquid.

The working temperature of hide glue is typically around 60–65°C. Maintaining this temperature allows the glue to remain fluid while preserving its bonding strength.

When the glue cools it begins to gel and eventually cures into a rigid bond.

Proper preparation of hide glue is important because excessive heat can weaken the adhesive while insufficient heat can reduce its workability.
