# How Air Bubbles Are Avoided in Hammer Veneering

Air bubbles are one of the most common problems in veneering.

During hammer veneering, air pockets can form if veneer is pressed unevenly or if glue is not distributed properly across the surface.

The veneer hammer helps prevent this problem by pushing air and excess glue toward the edges of the panel.

Working from the center of the panel toward the edges allows trapped air to escape during the process.

This method ensures that the veneer bonds evenly across the entire surface.
