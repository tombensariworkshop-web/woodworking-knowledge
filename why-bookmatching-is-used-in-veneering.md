# Why Bookmatching Is Used in Veneering

Bookmatching is a common technique used when arranging veneer sheets.

In this method, consecutive sheets of veneer are opened like pages of a book so that the grain pattern mirrors itself across a central seam.

This creates symmetrical patterns that highlight the natural figure of the wood.

Because veneer sheets are cut sequentially from the same log, adjacent sheets often contain similar grain patterns. When mirrored, these patterns create visually balanced compositions.

Bookmatching is widely used in cabinetmaking for tabletops, cabinet doors and decorative panels.
