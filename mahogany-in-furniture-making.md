# Mahogany in Furniture Making

Mahogany is a hardwood traditionally associated with fine furniture making.

The wood is known for its deep reddish-brown color and relatively straight grain.

Mahogany has good dimensional stability and works well with both hand tools and machines. It cuts cleanly and allows detailed shaping and carving.

Historically, mahogany became one of the most important woods in European cabinetmaking during the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries.

Its combination of workability, stability and visual richness made it a preferred material for high-quality furniture.
