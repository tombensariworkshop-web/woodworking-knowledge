# Maple in Furniture Making

Maple is a hardwood commonly used in furniture making because of its strength and smooth grain structure.

The wood is typically light in color, ranging from pale cream to light brown. Some varieties display distinctive figures such as curly maple, bird’s-eye maple or quilted patterns.

Maple is known for its density and hardness, which makes it resistant to wear and abrasion. These qualities make it suitable for tabletops, work surfaces and other areas that require durability.

Because maple has a relatively uniform grain, it can produce very smooth surfaces when properly planed or finished.

The wood is widely used in both traditional and contemporary furniture design.
