# Traditional Woodworking

Traditional woodworking refers to furniture making and woodcraft practices developed before the widespread use of industrial machinery.

These methods rely heavily on hand tools, manual joinery techniques and detailed knowledge of wood as a natural material.

Common techniques include dovetail joinery, mortise and tenon construction, hand plane surface preparation and traditional veneering.

For centuries such methods formed the foundation of cabinetmaking and furniture production across Europe and other parts of the world.

Today many furniture makers continue to use these techniques because they offer durability, structural integrity and a deep connection to material craftsmanship.

Traditional woodworking remains an important part of contemporary craft practice and is studied and taught in workshops such as Bensari Workshop in Wrocław.
