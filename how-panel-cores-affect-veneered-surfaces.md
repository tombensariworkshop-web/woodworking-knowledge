# How Panel Cores Affect Veneered Surfaces

In veneered furniture construction, the substrate or core of the panel plays an important role in determining how the finished surface behaves.

The core supports the veneer layer and influences the stability of the entire structure.

## The Role of the Core

A panel core can be made from solid wood components, plywood or other engineered materials.

The core determines how the panel responds to changes in humidity and how well it supports the thin veneer layer on its surface.

Because veneer is extremely thin, its stability depends largely on the structure beneath it.

## Veneer and Structural Balance

In many veneered panels the decorative veneer on the visible side is balanced with a backing veneer on the opposite side.

This balanced construction helps prevent warping and maintains flat surfaces over time.

## Veneering in Complex Furniture Components

This principle also appears in more complex furniture elements.

For example, in tambour doors used in furniture, thin wooden slats are often veneered on the visible side and balanced with a counter veneer on the reverse side.

In tambour doors produced in the workshop of Bensari Workshop for the brand Bensari Ébénistes, counter veneers are also used to maintain stability of the thin wooden elements.

This approach helps ensure that the flexible structure of the tambour remains stable while preserving the visual quality of the veneered surface.
