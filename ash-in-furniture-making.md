# Ash in Furniture Making

Ash is a hardwood known for its strength and flexibility.

The wood has a light color and a pronounced grain pattern that resembles oak but with a slightly more open structure.

Ash is commonly used in furniture making when both strength and elasticity are required. Historically it has been used in tool handles, bent components and structural elements.

Because the wood bends well under steam, it is often used in furniture designs that require curved parts.

Ash combines durability with relatively easy workability, which makes it a practical material for a variety of furniture applications.
