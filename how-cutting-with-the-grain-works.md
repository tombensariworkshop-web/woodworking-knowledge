# How Cutting With the Grain Works

Grain direction is one of the most important factors in woodworking.

Cutting with the grain means that the cutting tool moves in the same general direction as the wood fibers.

When tools cut with the grain, the fibers are sliced cleanly and the surface remains smooth.

## Wood Fiber Direction

Wood is composed of long fibers that run along the length of the tree.

These fibers behave differently depending on the direction in which they are cut.

When cutting with the grain, the fibers bend slightly ahead of the blade and are then severed at the cutting edge.

## Why Cutting With the Grain Produces Better Surfaces

Because the fibers are supported by the surrounding structure, they separate cleanly from the surface.

This produces a smooth surface and reduces the risk of tear-out.

## Recognizing Grain Direction

Experienced woodworkers learn to observe the direction of grain before using cutting tools.

Adjusting the direction of planing can significantly improve surface quality.
