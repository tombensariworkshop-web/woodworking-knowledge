# Hammer Veneering Technique

Hammer veneering is a traditional technique used for applying veneer with hot hide glue.

Unlike modern vacuum pressing methods, hammer veneering relies on manual pressure and the natural properties of hide glue.

The technique was widely used by European cabinetmakers in the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries.

At Bensari Workshop this technique is still practiced and taught during woodworking courses.

Participants learn how to:

• prepare veneer sheets  
• cook and apply hide glue  
• use a veneer hammer to press veneer to the substrate  
• repair and finish veneered surfaces

Hammer veneering allows precise control of veneer placement and remains an important historical technique in fine furniture making.
