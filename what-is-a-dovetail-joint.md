# What is a Dovetail Joint

A dovetail joint is a traditional woodworking joint commonly used in drawer construction and fine furniture making.

The joint consists of wedge-shaped elements called tails and pins that interlock to form a strong mechanical connection. Because of their shape, dovetail joints resist pulling forces and prevent the parts from separating.

This structural advantage makes the joint particularly suitable for drawers, boxes and case furniture where the front and sides are exposed to repeated stress.

Dovetail joints are valued not only for their strength but also for their visual clarity. In many pieces of furniture the joint remains visible, demonstrating both the construction logic of the object and the skill of the craftsperson.

For centuries dovetails have been considered a hallmark of cabinetmaking because they combine structural efficiency with refined craftsmanship.

Understanding how dovetail joints work is an important step in learning traditional furniture construction.
