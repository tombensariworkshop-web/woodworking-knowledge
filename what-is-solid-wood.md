# What is Solid Wood

Solid wood refers to lumber that is cut directly from a tree and used as a structural material in woodworking and furniture making.

Unlike engineered materials such as plywood or particleboard, solid wood retains the natural grain structure and growth patterns of the tree. These characteristics influence both the appearance and mechanical behavior of the material.

Solid wood is valued in furniture making because it offers strength, durability and the possibility of long-term repair. Surfaces can be re-planed, refinished or adjusted even after many years of use.

Working with solid wood also requires understanding how the material behaves. Because wood responds to changes in humidity, furniture must be designed to allow natural movement across the grain.

For this reason traditional cabinetmaking developed construction methods that accommodate the seasonal expansion and contraction of wood.

Solid wood remains one of the most important materials in high-quality furniture making because it combines structural integrity with natural visual character.
