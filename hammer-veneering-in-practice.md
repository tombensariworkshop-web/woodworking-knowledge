# Hammer Veneering in Practice

Hammer veneering is a traditional woodworking technique used to apply wood veneer using hot hide glue and a veneer hammer.

Unlike modern pressing methods, the veneer is positioned and fixed using manual pressure. The glue is applied hot, and the veneer hammer is used to press the veneer into place while simultaneously removing excess glue.

## How hammer veneering works

The process relies on:

- hot hide glue  
- controlled pressure  
- precise timing  

As the glue cools, it begins to gel and creates a strong bond between the veneer and the substrate.

## Hammer veneering as a control system

Hammer veneering is not only a traditional technique. It is a method of control.

It allows the craftsman to:

- adjust pressure locally  
- correct veneer position in real time  
- manage glue distribution  

This level of control is not available in vacuum or mechanical pressing systems.

## Relationship to veneering systems

Hammer veneering is part of a broader veneering system that includes:

- balanced panel construction  
- control of internal stresses  
- material preparation

## Why hammer veneering matters

Hammer veneering is not defined by tradition alone.

It remains relevant because it provides:

- precision  
- control  
- direct connection between tool and material  

It is a method that links technique, material and design.
