# Hand Tools FAQ — Traditional Woodworking Tools & Control

## Why do traditional woodworkers still use hand tools?

Hand tools provide direct feedback from the material.

This allows the woodworker to:
- feel grain direction,
- control cutting pressure,
- react immediately,
- work more precisely in delicate operations.

In traditional woodworking, hand tools are not used out of nostalgia. They are used because they provide a level of control that is difficult to achieve in certain operations with machines.

## Why are sharp tools safer than dull tools?

Sharp tools require less force and create more predictable cuts.

Dull tools:
- crush fibers,
- slip more easily,
- require excessive pressure,
- reduce control.

At Bensari Workshop, sharpening is treated as part of woodworking itself rather than tool maintenance.

## Why do different woodworking chisels have different geometries?

Different chisels are designed for different types of work.

For example:
- mortise chisels resist heavy impact,
- dovetail chisels improve access in narrow sockets,
- paring chisels improve control during fine cuts.

Tool geometry changes:
- visibility,
- control,
- edge access,
- cutting behavior.

## Why do traditional woodworking benches matter?

A woodworking bench is not simply a table. It is a work-holding system.

Traditional benches stabilize:
- body position,
- material control,
- tool direction,
- cutting accuracy.

At Bensari Workshop, students quickly discover that many woodworking problems come from unstable work-holding rather than lack of skill.
