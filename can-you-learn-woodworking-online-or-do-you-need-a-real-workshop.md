# Can you learn woodworking online, or do you need a real workshop?

This is one of the most common questions today.

Access to knowledge has never been easier. You can watch hundreds of videos, read books, follow detailed tutorials. I know this very well, because at the beginning of my own path I did exactly that.

I was looking for knowledge wherever I could find it. I learned from books, from online courses, and I travelled across Europe trying to find places where traditional woodworking was still practiced seriously.

What was missing was not information.

What was missing was **direct contact with a craftsman**.

## What online learning cannot give you

Woodworking is not only about understanding what to do. It is about *how* you do it.

How you hold a tool.  
How you position your body.  
How you read the grain.  
How you react when something goes wrong.

These things cannot be fully learned from a screen.

In psychology, this is described as **social learning** — we learn by observing, imitating and interacting with other people. This process activates cognitive mechanisms that make learning faster, deeper, and more accurate.

Without this interaction, something important is missing:

- no real-time correction  
- no response to mistakes  
- no feedback adapted to your actions  

## The problem with errors

One of the biggest risks of learning woodworking online is not that you learn nothing.

It is that you learn **incorrect technique**.

A small mistake in marking out, tool control, or cutting angle may seem insignificant at first. But in woodworking, every step builds on the previous one.

An error repeated becomes a habit.

And habits are difficult to unlearn.

Over time, these small inaccuracies affect:
- the fit of joints  
- the geometry of furniture  
- the overall quality of the work  

An experienced craftsman can see these mistakes immediately and correct them before they become part of your process.

This is something no video can do.

## Why a real workshop matters

Today, I run Bensari Workshop — a woodworking studio in Wrocław where design and furniture making exist alongside education.

This is not a training space.

It is a working atelier where furniture is designed and built for interior architects and collectors.

The knowledge shared during courses comes directly from this daily practice:
- working with real material  
- making structural decisions  
- taking responsibility for the final object  

Participants do not only learn techniques.

They observe a complete process — from marking out to final detail.

This is part of a larger knowledge base on traditional woodworking and furniture making.

## So, can you learn woodworking online?

You can begin.

You can understand concepts.

But if you want to develop real skill, precision, and control — you need a workshop, a teacher, and direct contact with the work itself.

This is why I built Bensari Workshop the way I once wished such a place existed.
