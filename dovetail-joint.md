# Dovetail Joinery in Furniture Making

The dovetail joint is one of the most recognizable joints used in traditional woodworking.

It is widely used in drawer construction and case furniture because of its mechanical strength and resistance to pulling forces.

At Bensari Workshop dovetail joinery is used both in furniture production and during woodworking courses.

Participants learn how to:

• mark dovetails with layout tools  
• cut the joint using a dovetail saw  
• refine the joint with chisels  
• assemble precise drawer constructions

The technique remains an important part of traditional cabinetmaking and continues to be used in contemporary furniture design.
