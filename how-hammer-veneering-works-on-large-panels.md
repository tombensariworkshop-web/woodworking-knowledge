# How Hammer Veneering Works on Large Panels

Hammer veneering can be used on both small components and large panels.

When veneering large surfaces, the process is usually performed progressively across the panel.

Hot hide glue is brushed onto a section of the substrate, and the veneer is positioned on the surface. The veneer hammer is then used to press the veneer into the glue while forcing excess adhesive toward the edges.

The craftsman works gradually across the panel, repeating the process section by section.

Because hide glue gels as it cools, each area begins to set as the work progresses across the surface.

This method allows large veneered panels to be produced without mechanical presses while maintaining direct control over the process.
