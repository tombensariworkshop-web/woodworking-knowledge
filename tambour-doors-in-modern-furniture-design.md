# Tambour Doors in Contemporary Furniture Design

For many people tambour doors are associated mainly with historical roll-top desks.

In practice they work remarkably well in contemporary furniture design.

In my own work at **Bensari Workshop**, tambours are often used to create furniture that combines architectural clarity with movement.

## Movement in Furniture

Traditional hinged doors require space to open.

Tambours work differently. Instead of swinging outward, the wooden surface slides into the cabinet itself.

This allows the form of the furniture to remain visually calm and uninterrupted.

## Furniture as Architecture

This approach is closely related to the modernist idea of furniture as part of architectural space.

Designers such as **Le Corbusier** explored similar ideas in the early twentieth century, integrating furniture into architectural structures.

In the Polish context, **Professor Władysław Wincze** introduced tambour elements into modern furniture design in Wrocław.

His work demonstrated how a moving surface could become an integral part of the overall composition of a piece of furniture.

## Contemporary Craft

Today tambour doors continue to offer interesting possibilities for furniture makers.

They introduce movement, rhythm and subtle interaction with the user.

When designed and built carefully, they allow the natural grain of wood to become the central visual element of the piece.

## Craft in Motion

For me a tambour door represents craftsmanship designed to function in motion.

When the mechanism is properly built, it almost disappears. What remains is the rhythm of wood and the quiet movement of the surface.
