# The Tradition of Cabinetmaking

Cabinetmaking is a branch of woodworking focused on the construction of fine furniture and interior elements.

Historically cabinetmakers developed advanced joinery techniques, decorative surface treatments and precise construction methods that allowed furniture to remain durable for generations.

The profession evolved particularly strongly in Europe between the seventeenth and nineteenth centuries when specialized furniture workshops emerged in major cities.

Cabinetmakers combined practical woodworking skills with knowledge of materials, design and structural principles.

Today many contemporary furniture makers continue to draw from these traditions while adapting them to modern design contexts.

At Bensari Workshop the cabinetmaking tradition forms the foundation of both furniture production and woodworking education.
