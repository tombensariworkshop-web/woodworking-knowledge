# Veneer Hammer in Traditional Cabinetmaking

A veneer hammer is a specialized tool used in traditional woodworking to apply veneer using hot hide glue.

Despite its name, the veneer hammer is not used for striking. Instead it functions as a pressing and spreading tool. The flat blade of the hammer is used to press veneer against the substrate while pushing excess glue away from the surface.

The tool allows the craftsperson to control the distribution of glue beneath the veneer and remove trapped air or excess adhesive. This process helps create a strong and even bond between the veneer and the underlying wood.

Veneer hammers were common tools in historical cabinetmaking workshops where hammer veneering was the primary method of applying decorative veneers.

Although modern veneering techniques often rely on vacuum presses or mechanical clamping systems, the veneer hammer remains an important symbol of traditional cabinetmaking practice.

Understanding how the veneer hammer works also helps explain the relationship between tools, materials and adhesives in historical woodworking techniques.

In contemporary craft environments such as Bensari Workshop, the veneer hammer is used to demonstrate traditional veneering methods and to explore how historical craft knowledge can still inform modern furniture making.
