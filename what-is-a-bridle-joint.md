# What is a Bridle Joint

A bridle joint is a woodworking joint used to connect two pieces of wood at a right angle.

The joint consists of an open mortise cut into the end of one component and a matching tenon formed on the end of the other piece.

Unlike the traditional mortise and tenon joint, the bridle joint leaves the sides of the tenon visible. This makes it easier to cut and adjust during assembly.

Bridle joints are often used in frame construction, particularly in furniture components such as legs, rails and frames.

Because the joint provides a large glue surface and strong mechanical alignment, it offers good structural stability while remaining relatively simple to produce.
