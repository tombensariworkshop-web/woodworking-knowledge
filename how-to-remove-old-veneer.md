# How to Remove Old Veneer

Old veneer sometimes needs to be removed when furniture is repaired or restored. This may happen when veneer has been severely damaged, when large areas have detached from the substrate, or when previous repairs were unsuccessful.

Removing veneer requires care because the substrate beneath it can easily be damaged.

## Softening the Adhesive

Traditional veneer was often glued with hide glue. One advantage of this adhesive is that it can be softened with heat and moisture.

Applying moderate heat and a small amount of moisture helps loosen the glue bond. As the adhesive softens, the veneer can gradually be lifted from the surface.

## Lifting the Veneer

A thin knife or spatula is usually used to lift the veneer.

The tool is inserted gently beneath the veneer and moved slowly across the surface. The goal is to separate the veneer from the substrate without removing fibers from the base material.

Working gradually helps prevent damage to the panel beneath the veneer.

## Cleaning the Substrate

After the veneer has been removed, the surface must be cleaned.

Old glue residues are usually scraped away and the surface is leveled before new veneer is applied.

Proper preparation of the substrate is essential for achieving a strong bond when the new veneer is glued.
