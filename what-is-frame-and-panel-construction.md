# What is Frame and Panel Construction

Frame and panel construction is a traditional woodworking method used to build doors, cabinets and furniture panels.

The structure consists of a rigid frame surrounding a floating panel placed in the center.

The frame typically includes vertical elements called stiles and horizontal elements called rails. These components are joined together using traditional joints such as mortise and tenon.

The panel is inserted into grooves inside the frame but is not glued in place. This allows the panel to expand and contract with changes in humidity without damaging the structure.

Frame and panel construction is widely used because it creates stable surfaces while accommodating natural wood movement.

This method has been a fundamental technique in cabinetmaking for centuries.
