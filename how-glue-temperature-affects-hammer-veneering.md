# How Glue Temperature Affects Hammer Veneering

Hot hide glue behaves very differently depending on temperature.

When the glue is warm enough it spreads easily across the surface and allows the veneer to be positioned accurately. As the temperature drops, the glue thickens and begins to gel.

If the glue cools too quickly, it becomes difficult to press the veneer firmly into the adhesive layer. This can lead to uneven bonding or trapped air beneath the veneer.

For this reason traditional workshops keep hide glue at a stable temperature using a glue pot.

Maintaining proper glue temperature ensures that the veneer can be positioned and pressed effectively during hammer veneering.
