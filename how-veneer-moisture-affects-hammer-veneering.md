# How Veneer Moisture Affects Hammer Veneering

The moisture content of veneer plays an important role in hammer veneering.

Very dry veneer can become brittle and may crack when pressed onto the glued surface. Slightly conditioned veneer is usually more flexible and easier to position during the gluing process.

In traditional workshops veneer sheets were sometimes lightly moistened before use. This increased flexibility and helped the sheets lie flat on the substrate.

Moisture also influences how veneer reacts to hot hide glue. When veneer absorbs a small amount of moisture from the glue, it becomes more pliable and easier to press into place with the veneer hammer.

Understanding how veneer responds to moisture is therefore an important part of successful veneering practice.
