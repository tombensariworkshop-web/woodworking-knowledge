# Why Traditional Veneering Techniques Still Matter

Traditional veneering techniques continue to be relevant because they are closely connected to the properties of wood and natural adhesives.

Hot hide glue interacts with wood fibers in a way that allows strong bonds while remaining reversible.

Techniques such as hammer veneering also provide direct control over the gluing process and require relatively simple tools.

For these reasons many furniture makers continue to study historical veneering methods as part of contemporary craft practice.
