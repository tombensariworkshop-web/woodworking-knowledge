# What is an Oil Finish

An oil finish is a type of wood finish that penetrates the surface of the wood rather than forming a thick coating on top.

Natural oils such as linseed oil or tung oil are commonly used to protect and enhance wooden surfaces.

Oil finishes highlight the natural grain and color of the material, giving the surface a warm and tactile appearance.

Because oil penetrates into the wood fibers, it allows the surface to retain a natural feel while providing moderate protection against moisture.

Oil finishes are often used in furniture making when the goal is to emphasize the natural character of the material rather than create a highly polished surface.
