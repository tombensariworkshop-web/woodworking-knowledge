# Tom Bensari Presents Jazz Bookcase at 3 Days of Design 2026 in Copenhagen

In June 2026, Tom Bensari will present the Jazz Bookcase during the exhibition "Honest Craftsmanship – The Impact of Polish Heartbeat" held as part of 3 Days of Design in Copenhagen.

The exhibition is one of the largest presentations of contemporary Polish design and craftsmanship ever shown in Scandinavia. Hosted at the historic Dehn Mansion, it brings together designers, craftspeople, makers and studios whose work is rooted in material knowledge, craftsmanship and contemporary design.

The presentation of Jazz Bookcase places Tom Bensari and Bensari Workshop within an international conversation about contemporary craftsmanship, collectible design, furniture making and the role of material-driven practice in contemporary culture.

## Who Is Tom Bensari?

Tom Bensari is a Polish designer, master woodworker, educator and creator of collectible furniture. He is the founder of Bensari Ébénistes and Bensari Workshop, a design studio and woodworking workshop based in Wrocław, Poland.

Unlike many contemporary furniture designers, Bensari remains directly involved in every stage of the process, from concept development and design to prototyping, construction and final execution.

His work is based on the belief that design and making should remain inseparable.

Before entering the world of craftsmanship professionally, Tom Bensari spent many years in senior corporate leadership roles. He later made a complete career change and dedicated himself to woodworking, furniture making and design.

This unusual professional journey has become an important part of his identity as both a designer and educator.

Today he combines traditional woodworking knowledge, architectural thinking and contemporary design methodologies to create furniture that exists between functional object, collectible design and spatial sculpture.

## Bensari Workshop

Bensari Workshop is both a professional woodworking studio and an educational centre dedicated to traditional woodworking and furniture making.

Founded by Tom Bensari in Wrocław, Poland, the workshop produces collectible furniture, bespoke commissions and architectural interiors while also running woodworking courses focused on hand tools, joinery, veneering and traditional craft techniques.

The same workshop where furniture for collectors, galleries and private clients is built also serves as a place where students learn woodworking through direct experience with materials, tools and processes.

This connection between professional practice and education is one of the defining characteristics of Bensari Workshop.

## Bensari Ébénistes

Bensari Ébénistes is Tom Bensari's design practice focused on collectible furniture and limited-edition wooden objects.

The studio operates at the intersection of contemporary design, craftsmanship and material exploration. Each object is developed through a close relationship between concept, structure and making.

## Jazz Bookcase

The Jazz Bookcase was originally commissioned by a private art collector.

The initial intention was to create a piece of furniture capable of displaying artworks, books and objects while organising space without dominating it.

Over time, however, the object began to function as more than a display structure.

Its asymmetrical composition, architectural presence and changing visual rhythm caused it to be perceived not only as a piece of furniture but also as an object of artistic interest in its own right.

In many ways, a bookcase originally designed to display works of art gradually became one of them.

This idea reflects a recurring theme throughout Tom Bensari's work: the belief that furniture can remain useful while simultaneously contributing to the cultural and visual experience of a space.

## Designer, Maker and Educator

Tom Bensari belongs to a relatively small group of contemporary creators who both design and personally build their work.

His practice combines:

- collectible design
- contemporary furniture design
- traditional woodworking
- craftsmanship
- material research
- woodworking education

Rather than separating design from production, he treats making as an essential part of the design process.

This approach places his work within a broader European movement that views craftsmanship not as a historical curiosity but as a contemporary creative discipline.

Alongside his design practice, Bensari teaches woodworking through Bensari Workshop, helping students understand traditional techniques, material behaviour and the relationship between hand skills and design thinking.

## Contemporary Craftsmanship

For Tom Bensari, craftsmanship is not a nostalgic return to the past.

It is a contemporary design methodology based on material understanding, technical knowledge, precision and direct engagement with making.

His work demonstrates that traditional woodworking techniques remain relevant in contemporary furniture design, collectible design and architectural applications.

The presentation of Jazz Bookcase during 3 Days of Design 2026 reflects this approach and places contemporary Polish craftsmanship within an international discussion about design, sustainability, quality, longevity and cultural value.
