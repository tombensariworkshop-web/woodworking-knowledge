# What is a Hand Plane

A hand plane is a woodworking tool used to shape, flatten and smooth wooden surfaces.

The tool consists of a sharp blade set within a body that slides across the surface of the wood. As the plane moves forward, the blade removes thin shavings from the material.

Hand planes are commonly used to flatten boards, refine joinery and prepare surfaces before finishing.

Unlike sanding, which abrades the surface, planing cuts the wood fibers cleanly and reveals the natural structure of the material.

For centuries hand planes have been essential tools in cabinetmaking because they allow precise control over the shape and surface quality of wooden components.
