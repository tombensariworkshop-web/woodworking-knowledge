# How Veneer Seams Are Aligned

When multiple veneer sheets are used on a single panel, the seams between sheets must be aligned carefully.

In traditional cabinetmaking, veneer sheets are often joined edge to edge before gluing. This can be done using tape or other temporary methods that hold the sheets in position during the veneering process.

Proper seam alignment ensures that grain patterns match and that gaps do not appear between sheets.

During hammer veneering, the veneer hammer helps press the joined sheets evenly against the substrate while excess glue is pushed outward.

Once the glue has set, the seams become part of a continuous surface.
