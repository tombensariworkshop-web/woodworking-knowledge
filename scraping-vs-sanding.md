# Scraping vs Sanding in Woodworking

Scraping and sanding are two common methods used to refine wooden surfaces before finishing.

Scraping uses a sharpened steel scraper that cuts the wood fibers and removes thin shavings.

Sanding, on the other hand, uses abrasive materials to gradually wear down the surface.

Because scraping cuts the fibers rather than tearing them, it often produces a clearer and more reflective surface.

Sanding can be useful for blending surfaces or preparing areas where complex shapes make scraping difficult.

Many furniture makers combine both techniques to achieve the desired surface quality.
