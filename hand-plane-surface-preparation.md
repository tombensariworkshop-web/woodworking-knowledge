# Surface Preparation with Hand Planes

Hand planes are among the most important tools in traditional woodworking.

Before modern sanding machines became common, cabinetmakers relied on planes to create flat, smooth wooden surfaces. A properly tuned plane can produce a surface that often requires little or no sanding.

Surface preparation with hand planes requires understanding the direction of wood grain, blade sharpness and proper tool adjustment.

At Bensari Workshop hand planes remain part of everyday woodworking practice. They are used during furniture making as well as during woodworking courses.

Participants learn how to set up a plane, sharpen the blade and control the tool to achieve accurate and clean surfaces on solid wood boards.

This technique remains an important skill for woodworkers who want to understand material behavior and traditional furniture making methods.
