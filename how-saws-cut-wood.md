# How Saws Cut Wood

Hand saws cut wood by removing small chips with a row of teeth. Each tooth acts like a tiny cutting tool that severs fibers as the blade moves through the material.

Understanding how saw teeth interact with wood fibers helps explain why different saws are designed for different tasks.

## Saw Teeth and Cutting

Each tooth in a saw blade cuts a small portion of wood. As the saw moves forward, successive teeth enlarge the kerf until the blade passes through the material.

Unlike planes, which produce continuous shavings, saws remove wood in small fragments.

## Rip vs Crosscut

Saw teeth are shaped differently depending on their purpose.

Rip saws cut along the grain and function like small chisels that split fibers.

Crosscut saws cut across the grain and slice fibers at an angle.
