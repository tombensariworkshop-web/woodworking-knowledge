# How Veneering Controls Wood Movement

One of the important functions of veneering is controlling the dimensional movement of wood.

Solid wood expands and contracts across the grain as humidity changes. When a thin veneer layer is applied to a stable substrate such as a panel, the movement of the decorative surface becomes much more limited.

Because veneer sheets are very thin, they respond differently to humidity than thick boards. When bonded to a stable substrate with hide glue, the veneer surface remains relatively stable.

This structural principle explains why veneering has been widely used in cabinetmaking for centuries.

Veneered panels allow furniture makers to create large decorative surfaces while minimizing the risks associated with solid wood movement.
