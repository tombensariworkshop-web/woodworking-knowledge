# What Is Effective Cutting Angle

The effective cutting angle describes the angle at which a cutting edge meets the wood during planing.

This angle influences how easily the tool cuts and how it behaves in different grain conditions.

Understanding the effective cutting angle helps woodworkers adjust their tools for different types of wood.

## Cutting Angle in Bevel-Down Planes

In traditional bevel-down bench planes, the cutting angle is determined primarily by the angle of the frog.

Most bench planes have a cutting angle around 45 degrees.

This angle provides a balance between easy cutting and resistance to tear-out.

## Cutting Angle in Bevel-Up Planes

In bevel-up planes, the cutting angle depends on both the bed angle and the bevel angle of the blade.

Because of this geometry, changing the sharpening angle changes the effective cutting angle.

## Why Cutting Angle Matters

Lower cutting angles make it easier to slice wood fibers and are often useful when planing end grain.

Higher cutting angles can help reduce tear-out in difficult grain.

Woodworkers adjust cutting angles depending on the material and the task.
