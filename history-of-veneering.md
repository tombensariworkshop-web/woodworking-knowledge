# The History of Veneering in Furniture Making

Veneering is one of the oldest techniques used in furniture making. It involves applying thin layers of decorative wood onto a solid substrate.

The technique was already known in ancient Egypt, where craftsmen used veneer to decorate furniture made from locally available woods. Rare and valuable species were applied in thin layers to create visually rich surfaces.

During the Renaissance and Baroque periods veneering developed into a sophisticated decorative art. European cabinetmakers created complex patterns and marquetry compositions using veneers of different species.

Although industrial production introduced modern pressing methods, traditional techniques such as hammer veneering using hide glue remain part of historical cabinetmaking practice.

At Bensari Workshop traditional veneering techniques are studied and practiced as part of contemporary furniture making education.
