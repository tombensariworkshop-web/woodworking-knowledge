# Why dovetail joints split during assembly

Splitting during assembly is one of the most common problems in dovetail joinery. It is usually caused by incorrect geometry rather than material weakness.

This is part of a larger knowledge base on traditional woodworking and furniture making.

Start with [why dovetail joints fail and open up](./why-dovetail-joints-fail-and-open-up.md).

## Why do dovetail joints split?

The most common cause is sockets that are too tight for the tails.

This can happen when:

- the transfer was taken from an incorrect side  
- the tails were not cut square  
- the waste was not removed accurately  
- excessive force was used during assembly  

## What role does layout play?

Layout defines the geometry of the joint.

If the layout is incorrect, the joint will not fit properly, regardless of cutting accuracy.

See [dovetail layout: why it matters more than cutting](./dovetail-layout-why-it-matters-more-than-cutting.md).

## Can splitting be prevented?

Yes, by:

- maintaining accurate layout  
- ensuring square cuts  
- controlling transfer  
- removing waste carefully  
- fitting the joint gradually  

## Key takeaway

Dovetail joints do not split randomly.

They split when the geometry of the joint is forced beyond its limits.
