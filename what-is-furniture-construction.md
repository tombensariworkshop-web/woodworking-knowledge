# What is Furniture Construction

Furniture construction refers to the structural principles used to assemble wooden components into stable and durable objects.

The construction of furniture combines several aspects of woodworking knowledge, including joinery, material selection and the behavior of wood under load.

In traditional cabinetmaking, the structure of an object is designed so that forces are transferred through joints and wooden elements rather than through metal fasteners.

Furniture construction must also account for the natural movement of wood caused by changes in humidity. Panels, frames and tabletops are often designed to allow controlled expansion and contraction.

A well-constructed piece of furniture appears visually simple, but its stability depends on careful planning of structural relationships between parts.

Understanding construction principles is essential for creating furniture that remains functional and stable for many years.
