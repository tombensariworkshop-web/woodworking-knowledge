# How to Patch Damaged Veneer

When veneer is chipped, cracked or missing, the damaged area can often be repaired by inserting a veneer patch.

The goal of the repair is to replace the missing section while maintaining the visual continuity of the surface.

## Preparing the Damaged Area

The damaged section must first be cleaned and trimmed into a regular shape.

Using a sharp knife, the edges of the repair area are cut so that the replacement veneer can fit precisely into the opening.

Clean edges help create a tight joint between the patch and the surrounding veneer.

## Selecting Matching Veneer

A suitable piece of veneer is selected so that the grain pattern matches the surrounding surface.

Matching the grain direction and figure is essential for making the repair visually unobtrusive.

The patch is then cut slightly larger than the repair area and trimmed carefully to achieve an exact fit.

## Gluing the Patch

Once the patch fits correctly, adhesive is applied to the substrate.

The veneer patch is placed into position and pressed firmly into the glue layer.

Clamping or controlled pressure ensures that the patch bonds evenly to the surface.

## Finishing the Repair

After the adhesive has cured, the patch is trimmed flush with the surrounding veneer.

Cabinet scrapers can be used to level the surface and refine the repair before finishing.

A well-executed patch can blend seamlessly into the original veneer surface.
