# What is Surface Finishing

Surface finishing refers to the final stage of woodworking in which protective or decorative coatings are applied to wood.

The purpose of finishing is to protect the surface from moisture, dirt and wear while enhancing the natural appearance of the material.

Before finishing is applied, the wood surface must be carefully prepared. This preparation may involve planing, scraping or light sanding to create a clean and even surface.

Common finishing materials include oils, waxes, varnishes and shellac. Each type of finish produces different visual and protective qualities.

Some finishes emphasize the natural grain of the wood, while others create a more uniform or glossy appearance.

Surface finishing plays an important role in furniture making because it determines how the material will look and perform during everyday use.
