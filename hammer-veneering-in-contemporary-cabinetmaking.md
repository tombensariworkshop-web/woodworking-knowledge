# Hammer Veneering in Contemporary Cabinetmaking

Although modern workshops often rely on vacuum presses and synthetic adhesives, hammer veneering remains a practical technique in contemporary cabinetmaking.

The method allows direct control over the process of gluing veneer. The craftsman can adjust the position of veneer sheets, remove excess glue and respond to changes in the material during the work.

This level of control is particularly valuable when working with decorative veneers or complex grain patterns.

In workshops such as Bensari Workshop the technique is taught not only as historical knowledge but as a practical approach to working with wood.
