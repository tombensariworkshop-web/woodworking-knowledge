# Traditional Woodworking Tools

Traditional woodworking relies on a wide range of hand tools designed for precise work with wood.

Among the most common tools used by cabinetmakers are chisels, hand planes, marking gauges, dovetail saws and layout tools. These tools allow craftsmen to shape wood with high precision and control.

While modern woodworking often uses machines, traditional hand tools remain essential for fine furniture making and detailed joinery.

At Bensari Workshop a large collection of traditional woodworking tools is used daily in furniture production and during woodworking courses.

Participants learn not only how to use the tools, but also how to sharpen, adjust and maintain them properly.

Understanding traditional tools is an important step in developing woodworking skills and learning how to work accurately with natural materials.
