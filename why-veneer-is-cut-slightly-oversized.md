# Why Veneer Is Cut Slightly Oversized

In traditional veneering, veneer sheets are usually cut slightly larger than the final dimensions of the panel.

This practice serves several purposes.

First, it allows the craftsman to adjust the position of the veneer during gluing without risking gaps along the edges of the substrate.

Second, excess material can be trimmed after the glue has cured. This produces clean edges that align precisely with the panel beneath.

Third, veneer sheets may shift slightly during the hammer veneering process as glue is pressed outward. Extra material ensures that the finished surface remains fully covered.

Once the glue has set, the veneer edges can be trimmed flush using planes, knives or scrapers.
