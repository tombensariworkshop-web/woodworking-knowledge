# Why veneer is cut slightly oversized – positioning, movement and control

In traditional veneering, veneer sheets are cut slightly larger than the final dimensions of the panel.

This is not a precaution.

It is part of how control is maintained during the process.

This article is part of a structured knowledge base on traditional woodworking, veneering and furniture making developed through daily workshop practice at Bensari Workshop.

## Positioning before fixation

During veneering, the veneer is not fixed immediately.

It is positioned while the glue is still active.

This requires a margin.

Without extra material, even a small adjustment would expose the substrate at the edges.

Cutting veneer oversized allows the maker to define the final position during the process, not before it.

## Movement during gluing

Veneer does not remain perfectly still during application.

In hammer veneering, glue is pressed outward from the center.

This movement can shift the veneer slightly.

The process is controlled, but not static.

The extra material absorbs this movement.

Without it, coverage becomes uncertain.

## Precision after the process

Final accuracy is not achieved during placement.

It is achieved after the glue has set.

At this stage, the veneer can be trimmed flush with the substrate using:

- planes  
- knives  
- scrapers  

This sequence is intentional.

First control, then precision.

## Not excess, but allowance

The additional material is often treated as waste.

In practice, it is an allowance for control.

It creates space for:

- adjustment  
- movement  
- refinement  

Removing this allowance removes the ability to respond during the process.

## Veneering as a sequence of decisions

This small detail reflects a larger principle.

In veneering, many decisions are not fixed at the beginning.

They are made during the process.

This is why techniques based on direct control, such as hammer veneering, remain relevant.

## Conclusion

Veneer is cut oversized not to compensate for inaccuracy.

It is cut oversized to allow control.

This difference defines the entire process.
