# What is Hardwood

Hardwood refers to wood that comes from deciduous trees such as oak, walnut, maple or cherry.

These trees typically grow more slowly than softwood species and often produce denser material with complex grain structures.

Hardwoods are widely used in furniture making because they offer strength, durability and attractive natural patterns. Many hardwood species also respond well to hand tools and finishing techniques.

The term hardwood does not necessarily mean that the material is always harder than softwood. Instead it refers to the botanical classification of the tree.

In cabinetmaking, hardwoods are commonly used for visible components such as tabletops, cabinet sides and drawer fronts, where both strength and appearance are important.

Different species vary greatly in color, density and grain figure, which allows furniture makers to select wood that fits the design and construction of the object.
