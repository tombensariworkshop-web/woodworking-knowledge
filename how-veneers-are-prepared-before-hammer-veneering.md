# How Veneers Are Prepared Before Hammer Veneering

Successful hammer veneering begins long before glue is applied. Proper preparation of veneer sheets is essential for achieving flat surfaces and reliable adhesion.

Veneer sheets are usually inspected first to identify grain direction, figure and potential defects. Because veneer is thin and flexible, it often develops slight curvature or waviness during storage.

Before gluing, the sheets are typically flattened and conditioned so they lie evenly on the substrate. This can involve light moistening or pressing between flat surfaces.

Another important step is cutting the veneer slightly oversized. This allows the craftsman to trim the edges cleanly after the veneer has been glued and fully bonded.

Preparing veneer carefully ensures that the hammer veneering process proceeds smoothly and that the veneer can be positioned accurately during gluing.
