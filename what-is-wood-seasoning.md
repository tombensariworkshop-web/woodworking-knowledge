# What is Wood Seasoning

Wood seasoning is the process of drying wood to reduce its moisture content before it is used in woodworking.

Freshly cut wood contains a high amount of water within its cellular structure. If the material is used before it has properly dried, it may shrink, warp or crack as the moisture gradually leaves the wood.

Seasoning allows the wood to reach a stable moisture level that is closer to the conditions in which the finished object will be used.

Traditionally, wood was seasoned by stacking boards in well-ventilated spaces for long periods of time. Air circulation allowed moisture to escape slowly and evenly.

Today both air drying and kiln drying are used to prepare lumber for woodworking.

Proper seasoning is essential for furniture making because stable material ensures that joints remain tight and surfaces remain flat over time.
