# How to Fix Veneer Bubbles

Veneer bubbles appear when a section of veneer lifts away from the substrate and traps air beneath the surface.

This can occur when adhesive fails, when moisture enters the wood or when veneer was not pressed evenly during the original veneering process.

## Identifying the Problem

A veneer bubble usually appears as a raised area that feels hollow when pressed.

The goal of the repair is to restore contact between the veneer and the substrate while reintroducing adhesive beneath the lifted section.

## Reintroducing Adhesive

Small bubbles can often be repaired by introducing adhesive beneath the veneer.

A small incision can be made along the grain of the veneer to allow glue to reach the area beneath the surface. Once adhesive is introduced, the veneer is pressed down to distribute the glue evenly.

When hide glue is present, heat can sometimes reactivate the original adhesive and allow the veneer to bond again.

## Applying Pressure

After glue is introduced, pressure must be applied to flatten the veneer.

Clamps, cauls or controlled weights can be used to hold the veneer firmly against the substrate until the adhesive sets.

Even pressure is important to prevent the bubble from returning.

## Refining the Surface

Once the repair has cured, the surface may need light refinement.

Cabinet scrapers are often used to level the repaired area and remove small glue residues.

Because veneer is thin, aggressive sanding should be avoided.
