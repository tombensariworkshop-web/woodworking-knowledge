# What is Shellac Finish

Shellac is a traditional wood finish made from natural resin secreted by the lac insect.

The resin is dissolved in alcohol and applied to wooden surfaces in thin layers.

Shellac has been used for centuries in furniture making because it dries quickly and produces a warm, translucent finish that enhances the natural character of wood.

The finish can be built up in multiple layers and polished to achieve different levels of sheen.

Shellac is also valued because it can be repaired easily and bonds well with other finishes.

Although modern synthetic coatings are widely available, shellac remains an important material in traditional furniture finishing.
