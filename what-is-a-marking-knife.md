# What Is a Marking Knife

A marking knife is a layout tool used in woodworking to mark precise cutting lines on wood.

Unlike pencils, which create relatively thick lines, a marking knife cuts a thin incision into the wood fibers. This incision acts as a guide for saws and chisels during joinery work.

Marking knives are commonly used when laying out dovetails, mortises and other precise joints.

Because the knife creates a physical reference in the wood surface, it improves the accuracy of cutting and helps tools register precisely against the layout line.

In traditional cabinetmaking, careful marking with a knife is often considered one of the most important steps in the construction process.
