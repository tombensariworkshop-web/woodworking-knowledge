# What is Scraping in Woodworking

Scraping is a woodworking technique used to refine wooden surfaces using a cabinet scraper.

The scraper removes very thin shavings from the wood, allowing the craftsperson to smooth areas where the grain may tear when using other tools.

Scraping is particularly useful when working with highly figured woods or surfaces with changing grain direction.

Unlike sanding, which abrades the surface, scraping cuts the fibers cleanly and leaves a surface that reflects light more evenly.

In traditional cabinetmaking scraping is often used as a final step before applying finish to the wood.
