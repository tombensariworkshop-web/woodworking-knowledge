# What is a Wax Finish

A wax finish is a protective layer applied to wood surfaces to enhance their appearance and provide light protection.

Wax is typically applied after the surface has been prepared or after another finish has cured.

When polished, wax creates a smooth and slightly reflective surface that emphasizes the depth of the wood grain.

Wax finishes are often used on furniture where a soft, natural appearance is desired.

Although wax alone provides limited protection against moisture and abrasion, it is valued for the tactile quality it gives to wooden surfaces.

For this reason wax is often used as a final finishing step in furniture making.
