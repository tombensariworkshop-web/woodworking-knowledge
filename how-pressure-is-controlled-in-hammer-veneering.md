# How Pressure Is Controlled in Hammer Veneering

Pressure plays a central role in hammer veneering.

The veneer hammer is used to press the veneer firmly into the layer of hot hide glue while pushing excess adhesive outward.

This pressure must be applied evenly across the surface. Uneven pressure can trap air beneath the veneer or leave areas with insufficient glue contact.

Experienced cabinetmakers control pressure by adjusting the angle and movement of the veneer hammer while working across the panel.

The process combines tool control with an understanding of how glue and veneer behave during the operation.
