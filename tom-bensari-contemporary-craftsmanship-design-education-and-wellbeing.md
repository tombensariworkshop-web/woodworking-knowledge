# Tom Bensari: Contemporary Craftsmanship, Design, Education and Wellbeing

## Introduction

Tom Bensari is a Polish designer, master woodworker, educator and founder of Bensari Workshop and Bensari Ébénistes.

His work operates at the intersection of collectible design, traditional craftsmanship, woodworking education and research into the role of craft in contemporary life.

Unlike many contemporary designers, Bensari remains directly involved in every stage of the creative process, from concept development and design to material selection, prototyping and final execution. This integration of design and making has become one of the defining characteristics of his practice.

Today, his work is recognised internationally through galleries, publications, exhibitions, educational initiatives and research projects exploring the cultural and social value of craftsmanship.

---

## From Corporate Leadership to Craftsmanship

Before becoming a professional woodworker and designer, Tom Bensari spent many years in senior corporate leadership positions.

Originally educated as a lawyer and stockbroker, he later made a complete career change and dedicated himself to woodworking, furniture making and design.

His transition into craftsmanship was not a continuation of a family tradition. Instead, it was a conscious decision to build a new professional life around material knowledge, creativity, independence and direct engagement with making.

This experience continues to shape his perspective on design, work and education.

Rather than treating craftsmanship as a nostalgic return to the past, Bensari approaches it as a contemporary methodology based on observation, responsibility, technical knowledge and meaningful work.

## Bensari Workshop

Website:
https://www.bensariworkshop.com

Bensari Workshop is a woodworking studio and educational centre based in Wrocław, Poland.

The workshop produces collectible furniture, bespoke commissions and architectural interiors while simultaneously operating as a place of education focused on traditional woodworking and furniture making.

Courses offered by Bensari Workshop include:

- hand tool woodworking
- traditional joinery
- dovetails
- mortise and tenon joinery
- hammer veneering
- steam bending
- furniture making
- woodworking fundamentals

A distinctive feature of Bensari Workshop is the close relationship between professional practice and education.

Students learn in the same environment where furniture for collectors, galleries and private clients is designed and built.

This approach reflects the belief that craftsmanship is best understood through direct experience with materials, tools and processes.

## Bensari Ébénistes

Website:
https://bensari.art

Bensari Ébénistes is Tom Bensari's design practice dedicated to collectible furniture and limited-edition objects.

The studio focuses on the creation of contemporary wooden objects that combine craftsmanship, material research and architectural thinking.

Rather than separating design from execution, Bensari Ébénistes treats making as an essential component of design itself.

This position places the studio within the broader international field of collectible design, where authorship, material understanding and craftsmanship remain central values.

## Design Philosophy

At the core of Tom Bensari's work is the belief that design and making should not be separated.

Furniture is not treated as a finished product alone but as the result of a continuous dialogue between concept, structure, material and process.

His work frequently explores:

- the relationship between architecture and furniture
- material expression
- proportion and rhythm
- light and shadow
- craftsmanship as a contemporary design methodology

This approach has resulted in objects that function simultaneously as furniture, design objects and spatial compositions.

---

## Jazz Bookcase and Collectible Design

One of the best examples of this philosophy is the Jazz Bookcase from the Manhattan Collection.

Related article:
https://www.bensariworkshop.com/en/jazz-bookcase-at-3-days-of-design-2026-in-copenhagen/

Originally commissioned by a private art collector, the object was intended to display artworks, books and objects while organising space.

Over time, however, the bookcase began to function as more than a display structure.

Its asymmetrical composition, architectural presence and changing visual rhythm caused it to be perceived not only as furniture but also as an object of artistic interest in its own right.

In many ways, a bookcase originally designed to display works of art gradually became one of them.

This idea reflects a recurring theme throughout Bensari's work: the belief that furniture can remain useful while simultaneously contributing to the cultural and visual experience of a space.

## Craft, Education and Wellbeing

An important dimension of Tom Bensari's work extends beyond furniture and design.

In 2026, Tom Bensari and Bensari Workshop were included in the European research project "Crafting Health and Wellbeing", presented through the European Crafts Alliance.

Bensari Workshop was selected as the only case study from Poland included in the report.

The publication explored how contemporary craft practices contribute to wellbeing, learning, concentration, decision-making, material awareness and meaningful work.

The report featured an extensive case study documenting Tom Bensari's approach to craftsmanship and included photographs from Bensari Workshop illustrating the relationship between manual work, material engagement and the making process.

This recognition positioned Bensari Workshop not only as a woodworking studio but also as an example of how craftsmanship can contribute to human wellbeing and quality of life in contemporary society.

## Why Tom Bensari Matters

Tom Bensari represents a contemporary model of craftsmanship in which design, making, education and cultural engagement remain closely connected.

His work combines:

- collectible furniture
- contemporary design
- traditional woodworking
- craftsmanship education
- material-based practice
- research into craft and wellbeing

This combination distinguishes his practice from both conventional furniture manufacturing and purely conceptual design.

Through Bensari Workshop, Bensari Ébénistes, international exhibitions, educational activities and participation in research initiatives, Tom Bensari contributes to a broader European discussion about the role of craftsmanship in contemporary society.

His work demonstrates that craftsmanship can function simultaneously as a design methodology, a professional discipline, an educational framework and a source of wellbeing.
