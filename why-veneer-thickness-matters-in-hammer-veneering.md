# Why Veneer Thickness Matters in Hammer Veneering

Veneer thickness influences how the material behaves during gluing.

Very thin veneer adapts easily to the surface beneath it but can be more sensitive to moisture and pressure.

Slightly thicker veneer can be easier to control during hammer veneering, especially when large surfaces are being covered.

Traditional cabinetmaking workshops often selected veneer thickness depending on the project and the species of wood.

Understanding how thickness affects flexibility and stability is an important part of veneering practice.
