# What Makes Well-Crafted Furniture Durable

Durability in furniture depends on structure, material and workmanship.

Well-crafted furniture uses appropriate joinery, considers wood movement and maintains precision in construction. Strength does not come from mass, but from the relationship between elements.

## Structure and joinery

The durability of a piece of furniture is largely determined by how its parts are connected.

Among the most important structural joints are:

- mortise and tenon joinery  
- dovetail joints  

These joints define how forces are transferred and how the object behaves over time. When executed correctly, they provide strength without relying on mechanical fasteners.

## Material and movement

Wood is not static.

It expands and contracts depending on humidity and environment. Durable furniture accounts for this movement at every stage of construction.

This includes:
- correct grain orientation  
- appropriate joinery  
- balanced structures  

More about material:
[Why do furniture makers use veneer](./why-do-furniture-makers-use-veneer.md)

## Precision and execution

Durability depends on accuracy.

Even well-designed joints will fail if they are poorly executed. Precision in layout, cutting and fitting determines how well elements work together.

## A system, not a feature

Durability is not a single feature that can be added at the end.

It is the result of a system:
- structure  
- material  
- execution  

Each of these must be considered together.
