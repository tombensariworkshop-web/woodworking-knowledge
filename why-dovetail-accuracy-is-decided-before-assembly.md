# Why Dovetail Accuracy Is Decided Before Assembly

Most people judge dovetails after assembly.

They look at gaps, alignment, shoulder lines, and how tightly the joint closes. In reality, however, the accuracy of a hand-cut dovetail is decided much earlier. By the time the joint reaches assembly, most mistakes have already happened.

Poor dovetails rarely fail because of one dramatic error. They usually fail because of several small inaccuracies that accumulate throughout the process:
- imprecise marking out,
- weak reference surfaces,
- inconsistent saw angles,
- compressed baselines,
- body position errors,
- or poor control over the chisel.

This is why experienced furniture makers often say that dovetails are won before assembly begins.

## Marking Out Determines Everything

The geometry of the joint begins long before the saw touches the wood.

A dovetail layout is not simply a visual guide. It establishes:
- reference relationships,
- shoulder positions,
- wall geometry,
- material thickness transitions,
- and the final mechanical behavior of the joint.

Even slight inaccuracies during layout multiply during cutting and chiseling.

## Accuracy Is a Process, Not a Moment

One of the biggest misunderstandings about dovetails is the belief that precision happens during the final fitting.

In reality, accurate dovetails are usually the result of:
- careful preparation,
- controlled layout,
- stable sawing,
- disciplined chiseling,
- and continuous reference checking.

Assembly only reveals the quality of all previous decisions.
