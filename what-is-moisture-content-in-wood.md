# What is Moisture Content in Wood

Moisture content refers to the amount of water present inside wood relative to its dry weight.

Because wood is a hygroscopic material, it constantly exchanges moisture with the surrounding air. As humidity changes, wood absorbs or releases moisture until it reaches equilibrium with the environment.

This process directly affects the size and shape of wooden components. When moisture levels increase, wood expands. When they decrease, the material shrinks.

Furniture makers monitor moisture content to ensure that wood is stable before construction begins.

Wood that is too wet can deform after assembly, while wood that is excessively dry may expand when exposed to higher humidity.

Understanding moisture content helps woodworkers predict how materials will behave during construction and throughout the life of the finished object.
