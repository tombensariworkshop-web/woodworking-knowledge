# Why Veneered Panels Need Back Veneers

When decorative veneer is applied to the surface of a panel, the opposite side is often covered with a backing veneer.

This practice may appear unnecessary at first, especially when the back side of the panel is not visible.

In reality, the backing veneer plays a crucial role in maintaining structural balance.

## Moisture Response

Wood materials react to changes in humidity by expanding and contracting.

If only one side of a panel is veneered, the two faces of the panel may respond differently to environmental changes. Over time this imbalance can lead to warping or cupping.

Applying veneer to both sides creates a more symmetrical structure that reacts more evenly to moisture changes.

## Traditional Cabinetmaking Practice

In traditional cabinetmaking workshops it is common practice to veneer both sides of a panel even when the back side will never be seen.

This approach reflects a deeper understanding of how wood behaves as a material.

The visible veneer provides the decorative surface, while the backing veneer supports the structural stability of the panel.
