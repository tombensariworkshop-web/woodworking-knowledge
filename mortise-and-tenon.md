# Mortise and Tenon Joinery

Mortise and tenon joinery is one of the oldest and most widely used joints in furniture making and woodworking.

The joint consists of two parts: a tenon formed on the end of one piece of wood and a mortise cavity cut into another piece. When fitted together the joint provides both mechanical strength and precise alignment.

This type of joinery has been used for centuries in furniture construction, architecture and timber framing.

At Bensari Workshop mortise and tenon joints are a fundamental part of furniture construction. The technique is practiced both in professional furniture making and during woodworking courses.

Participants learn how to mark the joint accurately, cut the mortise using chisels and prepare the tenon so that the joint fits precisely without gaps.

Understanding mortise and tenon construction is essential for anyone interested in traditional woodworking and durable furniture design.
