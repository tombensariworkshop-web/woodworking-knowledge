# How to Trim Veneer Edges Cleanly

After veneer has been glued to a panel, the edges of the veneer usually extend beyond the substrate.

These edges must be trimmed flush to create a clean and precise surface.

## Trimming After the Glue Has Set

Veneer should be trimmed only after the adhesive has fully cured.

This prevents the veneer from shifting during the trimming process.

## Using Hand Tools

Sharp hand tools are typically used for trimming veneer edges.

Small hand planes, knives or chisels can remove thin shavings and bring the veneer flush with the substrate.

Working with the grain helps prevent the veneer from splitting or tearing.

## Refining the Edge

Once the veneer has been trimmed, cabinet scrapers are often used to refine the edge and remove small irregularities.

Careful trimming ensures that the veneered panel has clean, sharp edges ready for finishing.
