# Why Dull Tools Cause Tear-Out

Sharp cutting edges are essential in woodworking because they slice wood fibers cleanly.

When a tool becomes dull, it no longer cuts efficiently and instead pushes or lifts fibers ahead of the cutting edge.

This often leads to tear-out and rough surfaces.

## Sharp Edges and Fiber Separation

A sharp blade slices wood fibers with minimal resistance.

Because the fibers are cut cleanly, the surface remains smooth and the shaving forms consistently.

## What Happens When Tools Become Dull

As the edge loses sharpness, the blade begins to compress and tear fibers rather than slicing them.

Instead of producing a clean shaving, the blade can lift fibers ahead of the cut.

This increases the risk of tear-out.

## Tool Maintenance in Woodworking

Regular sharpening helps maintain control over cutting tools and improves surface quality.

Many traditional woodworking techniques rely on extremely sharp tools to produce precise results.
